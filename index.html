What is the ISS?

The ISS

The ISS (International Space Station) is a collaborative project among various countries, serving as a space research laboratory. It orbits Earth at about 420 kilometers, with astronauts conducting experiments in various scientific fields. The modular spacecraft has been continuously inhabited since 2000, allowing for long-term research and living in space.





Who owns the ISS?

The ISS is owned by various different countries

The ownership and operation of the International Space Station (ISS) involve a collaborative effort among multiple space agencies. The participating agencies include NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration) from the United States, Roscosmos (Russian Federal Space Agency) from Russia, ESA (European Space Agency) representing Europe, JAXA (Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency) from Japan, and CSA (Canadian Space Agency) from Canada. Together, these agencies contribute to the maintenance, research, and overall functioning of the ISS, making it a symbol of international cooperation in space exploration.
